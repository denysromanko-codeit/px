Designing a type scale that actually scales

Design Team · 6 min read · Updated today

A type scale only works when the styles sit next to each other without fighting. This page puts every style in a real layout so you can judge the rhythm, not the spec sheet.

The goal is a clear hierarchy: a reader should feel the jump from a title to body text instantly, and paragraphs should breathe without drifting apart.

Why line-height matters most

Size sets the hierarchy, but line-height sets the comfort. Toggle compact and comfortable above to feel how the body paragraphs change while everything else holds still.

A note on density

Compact line-height suits dense product UI; comfortable suits long-form reading. Body Small is the most sensitive — watch it closely in both modes.

Caption — Body Small is also the right size for footnotes, metadata, and helper text that sits under primary content.
